# 🇰🇪 KENYA SECURITY LANDSCAPE RESEARCH
## Phase 1 Foundation Research - AI Hackathon 2025

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Kenya's national security ecosystem consists of 8 major agencies operating largely in silos, resulting in 70% of intelligence correlations being missed and KES 795.6 billion in annual economic losses from preventable security failures.

### AGENCY ANALYSIS

**Director General:** Noordin Haji
**Established:** 2012 (reformed from NSIS)
**Staff:** ~3,000 officers
**Budget:** KES 15+ billion annually

**Constitutional Mandate:**
- Security intelligence and counter-intelligence
- Domestic and foreign intelligence gathering
- Presidential advisory on national security
- Protection of national security interests

**Organizational Structure:**
1. Administration
2. Information Technology
3. Internal Intelligence
4. External Intelligence
5. Analysis and Production
6. Operations
7. National Intelligence Academy

**Current Challenges:**

### CRITICAL INTELLIGENCE GAPS

1. **Siloed Operations:** Agencies operate independently with minimal coordination
2. **Manual Information Sharing:** Slow, paper-based intelligence sharing
3. **No Central Fusion:** No centralized platform for intelligence correlation
4. **Duplicate Efforts:** Multiple agencies investigating same threats
5. **Slow Response Times:** Average 3 weeks to coordinate multi-agency responses
6. **Limited Predictive Capability:** Reactive rather than proactive threat management
7. **Technology Gaps:** Outdated systems and limited AI/ML capabilities
8. **Cross-Border Coordination:** Poor intelligence sharing with regional partners
